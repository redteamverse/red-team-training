CFP REQUIREMENTS

What We Do Not Accept

AltSecCON focuses on practical, technical, and research-driven security content. To maintain a high-quality experience for attendees, we generally do not accept submissions that are purely promotional, lack technical depth, or do not provide actionable learning outcomes.

Sales & Product Pitches

Sessions designed primarily to promote products, services, vendors, or commercial offerings.

High-Level Awareness Talks

Generic overviews that lack demonstrations, technical depth, practical methodologies, or research findings.

Repeated Public Content

Talks that simply repeat widely available material without adding new perspectives, discoveries, or practical value.

No Clear Takeaways

Submissions that do not explain what attendees will learn or how the research improves security understanding.

The strongest AltSecCON submissions combine original research, technical depth, practical demonstrations, and actionable lessons for security practitioners.

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

What to Include in Your CFP Submission

To help our review committee evaluate your proposal effectively, please include the following information when submitting your CFP.

Title

A concise and compelling title that clearly reflects your session.

Abstract

A summary explaining the topic, research, techniques, and expected takeaways.

Detailed Outline

A breakdown of the session structure, key sections, demonstrations, and flow.

Difficulty Level

Indicate whether the session is Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced.

Speaker Bio

Name, background, experience, and relevant professional information.

Speaker Additional Information
  • Speaker headshot (high-quality photo)
  • Previous presentation history (if presented before)
  • Previous speaking experience
  • Supporting slide decks
  • Whitepapers and research reports
  • Source code, tooling, or proof-of-concept material

The following materials help the review committee gain a deeper understanding of your research, technical methodology, presentation approach, and experience.

TECHNICAL REVIEW COMMITTEE

How Our Review Committee Evaluates Research

Every CFP is reviewed by security researchers and practitioners who evaluate technical quality, originality, practical value, and relevance to enterprise security professionals.

AltSecCON
Review Committee

Technical Depth

We look for sessions that provide meaningful technical insights, advanced techniques, practical demonstrations, and knowledge that security professionals can apply in real-world environments.

Original Research

Strong submissions include new discoveries, novel attack or defensive techniques, unique research findings, or fresh perspectives on existing security challenges.

Practical Impact

Research should provide actionable lessons, realistic examples, or methodologies that can help Red Teams, defenders, and enterprise security teams improve their security posture.

Quality & Clarity

A strong CFP clearly explains the problem, research approach, methodology, demonstrations, and key takeaways attendees will gain from the session.

Security Relevance

Sessions should be relevant to modern security challenges including offensive security, detection engineering, cloud, identity, and enterprise defense.

Community Value

We value research that helps the wider security community, encourages knowledge sharing, and provides practical learning for professionals at different experience levels.

Submission Guidelines

Terms & Conditions

Please review the submission and speaker requirements before submitting your proposal. By submitting a proposal, you agree to the terms outlined below.

Proposal Terms

  • Submissions featuring original research, tools, techniques, or live demonstrations will be prioritized.
  • Each individual may submit up to three unique proposals.
  • AI/LLM-generated content is not permitted. AI may only be used to refine or polish author-written material.
  • All submitted proposals and supporting materials will be accessible to the Review Board.
  • Speakers are expected to respond promptly to clarification requests from the Review Board.
  • Acceptance and rejection notifications will be issued on a rolling basis.

Terms Upon Acceptance

  • Speakers grant Altered Security permission to record, reproduce, distribute, promote, and publish presentations in audio, video, and other media formats.
  • Speaker substitutions are not permitted without prior approval and will only be considered in the event of a medical or health emergency.
  • If a presentation is determined to contain plagiarism, AI-generated content, or promotional/vendor-focused material, Altered Security reserves the right to cancel the session and withdraw any associated honorarium.
