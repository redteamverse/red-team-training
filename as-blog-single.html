Understanding Enterprise AD CS Attack Paths

Active Directory Certificate Services (AD CS) remains one of the most commonly overlooked enterprise attack surfaces. Misconfigured templates, dangerous enrollment permissions, and weak certificate mapping controls allow attackers to escalate privileges, impersonate users, and achieve long-term persistence inside enterprise Active Directory environments.

This research explores real-world attack techniques attackers use during enterprise red team operations to abuse AD CS infrastructure and compromise domain environments.

Exploiting Vulnerable Enterprise Certificate Templates

Attackers frequently target enterprise certificate templates configured with weak enrollment permissions and dangerous EKU settings. By abusing ESC1 or ESC2 style template misconfigurations, attackers can request certificates on behalf of privileged accounts and authenticate directly to Active Directory services.

Modern enterprise environments often deploy Active Directory Certificate Services without fully understanding the impact of dangerous certificate template configurations. During red team operations, AD CS becomes one of the most effective attack surfaces for privilege escalation, persistence, and stealthy authentication abuse.

“Certificate-based persistence remains one of the stealthiest enterprise persistence mechanisms in modern Active Directory environments.”

During adversary simulations, these attack paths are often combined with Kerberos abuse, delegation attacks, and lateral movement tradecraft to compromise domain environments while maintaining operational stealth.

Understanding ESC1 Template Misconfigurations

ESC1 vulnerabilities occur when certificate templates allow low-privileged users to supply arbitrary Subject Alternative Names (SANs). Attackers can abuse this configuration to request certificates impersonating privileged users such as Domain Admins or Enterprise Administrators.

Once a malicious certificate is issued, attackers can authenticate through Kerberos PKINIT and gain access to enterprise resources without ever knowing the target account password. This dramatically reduces operational noise compared to traditional credential theft techniques.

Dangerous Enrollment Permissions in Enterprise PKI

Many organizations unintentionally expose dangerous enrollment permissions to Authenticated Users or Domain Users groups. Combined with weak template security settings, this allows attackers to enroll certificates capable of client authentication or smartcard logon operations.

Enterprise PKI environments frequently inherit legacy template configurations that remain active for years without security reviews. During red team engagements, these outdated templates often provide direct paths to enterprise privilege escalation. 

certipy find -u user@corp.local -p Password123
certipy req -template VulnerableTemplate
certipy auth -pfx administrator.pfx

Enterprise Attack Paths & Lateral Movement

Certificate-based authentication enables attackers to move laterally across enterprise environments without triggering traditional password reset or Kerberos pre-authentication alerts. This provides highly stealthy operational tradecraft during adversary simulations.

Attackers commonly combine AD CS abuse with Kerberos delegation attacks, constrained delegation abuse, and shadow credentials to build resilient persistence mechanisms across enterprise domains.

Persistence Through Certificate Abuse

Unlike passwords, certificates often remain valid for extended periods, enabling attackers to maintain access even after password resets or account remediation efforts. This creates significant challenges for blue teams attempting to fully evict adversaries from enterprise environments.

Attackers frequently store malicious certificates offline and reintroduce them later during follow-on operations. In mature enterprise environments, certificate-based persistence can remain undetected for long periods without dedicated PKI monitoring capabilities.

Operational Security During Red Team Engagements

Mature red team operators avoid noisy exploitation techniques that trigger authentication anomalies or password reset events. Certificate-based authentication enables stealthier access patterns while blending into legitimate enterprise authentication traffic.

Operational security considerations include limiting certificate requests, avoiding abnormal template usage patterns, and carefully managing issued certificate lifetimes during long-term enterprise operations.

Detection Engineering & Defensive Strategies

Defenders should continuously audit certificate templates, enrollment permissions, EKU configurations, and CA security settings. Monitoring certificate issuance events and unusual PKINIT authentication patterns is critical for identifying enterprise certificate abuse.

Blue teams should also monitor for suspicious certificate enrollments originating from low-privileged accounts or systems outside normal enrollment baselines. Enterprise PKI monitoring remains an essential capability for defending against modern Active Directory attack paths.

Audit all enterprise certificate templates
Remove dangerous enrollment permissions
Monitor PKINIT authentication anomalies
Review long-lived certificate validity periods
