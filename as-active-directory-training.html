Why Enterprise Teams Need Active Directory Security Training

Active Directory is still one of the most important identity systems inside modern organizations. When AD is misconfigured or poorly monitored, attackers can chain together excessive privileges, weak delegation, exposed credentials, Kerberos abuse, and trust relationships to move deeper into the environment.

This training helps teams understand those attack paths in a controlled lab so they can improve assessment methodology, monitoring visibility, hardening priorities, and defensive response.

Enterprise AD Attack Paths

Understand how domains, forests, users, groups, sessions, ACLs, and privileges connect to create real attack paths inside Active Directory environments.

Kerberos and Delegation Risk

Learn how Kerberos tickets, delegation settings, service accounts, and authentication flows can create abuse paths and defensive monitoring opportunities.

Trust Relationships and Privilege Exposure

Identify how domain trusts, forest trusts, excessive privileges, and weak segmentation can expand the impact of an AD compromise.

PowerShell and Native Tradecraft

Understand how built-in Windows capabilities are used during authorized assessments, and how defenders can reason about visibility, controls, and detection.
